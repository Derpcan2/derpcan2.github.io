Sword of Darkness
...

Details

Traits
Magic Type: Necrotic
Category: Weapon
Owner: Flint's Father (formerly), Flint (formerly) Flame Knight(formerly)
Abilities
Mind Manipulation of the Host
Enhancing the Host Physically
Creation of Matter through the Host's Will
Limited Movement on its own
Protecting itself

A mysterious blade of unknown origin. From what I can gather, it was hidden away in a cave near Faylil and discovered by Coal, who was Flint's father. When he picked it up, it corrupted him and made him angry, violent, and bitter.

After being defeated, he dropped the blade. The team of heroes attempted to join together to pick it up, but it ended up corrupting Sawyer. Instead of bitter and angry, though, it seemed to latch on to Sawyer's desire to protect and project it onto the sword. The sword also has the strange ability to manifest desires physically to some extent. In Sawyers's case, it enhanced his bo-staff with spikes and sharp edges. After subduing the out-of-control Sawyer, the sword landed on the ground.

The group attempts to save Flint's father by destroying the sword, but it has some kind of force field protecting it. The group focuses their attention on the crystal in the middle of the sword.

They eventually smash it, but the sword begins to freak out instead of saving Cole. Purple tendrils extend from the place where the crystal was and engulf the sword. It hovers into the air, and begins flying away. Milly attempts to pull it back with a thorn whip, but it doesn't work.

Flint's Father passes away. I've done some thinking, and a combination of the sword possessing him (as he wasn't bonded, more on that later), the battle with the heroes, and his general frailness led to his demise.

Around a month later, a bitter, angry Flint seeks out the sword. He lifts it up, and because he sought out the sword deliberately, he bonds with the blade. Not only does this prevent the physical strain the sword puts on unwilling hosts, but it actually enhances the sword's abilities. I believe that Flint had a deep desire to become a Hero, to prove his worth, and to be respected. So the sword twisted this desire and turned him into the violent and powerful Flame Knight. I believe the bonding allowed the sword to create a larger-scale physical manifestation of its host's will, and thus, the sword created the Flame Knight Armor.

After eventually being defeated, Flint is knocked unconscious, and his arm is cut off, severing the connection to the sword. The team finds a safe way to carry the sword for now. I hope they find a safe way to destroy this.
