Music to my Ears

I think it's been made pretty apparent that I'm very deliberate with my music choices. Often, music actually fuels my ideas for stories or character motivations. Many events you've experienced have come to be simply because a certain song gave me a vibe or feeling that inspired me to find a way to incorporate it. So I wanted to go into a couple music choices and explain them a bit further.

Lyrical Relevance
...

Many of the tracks I've picked were chosen for the lyrics they contain.

  • The main theme of the campaign Don't Let the Party Die by Sleeping with Sirens (who's lead singer also did the Sonic Frontiers boss themes by the way) is pretty obvious. It is a song about doing what you can to not let the party die (though I think I recontextualized it a little bit).
  • Find your Flame from Sonic Frontiers, which serves as the theme of the Flame Knight, is actually what inspired the character. Beyond the obvious connection of the word "Flame", the song kinda represents how Flint feels. While also showing the ruthlessness and twisted desires caused by the Sword of Darkness.
    "We won't back down
    We won't (yeah, yo)
    I'm here to go beyond my limit, not to compromise
    And you can tell me if I mean it looking in my eyes"
    • The intro of song kinda shows how Flint refuses to hold back on anyone now that he has the power to win. He's strong now and he refuses to hide it.
      "I can cut right through steel, I can bury the pain
      So go down for a second, just get out of my way (out of my way)
      You cheat, and you steal, I'm not letting it go
      So take this as a lesson, 'cause that's all that we know"
    • This part references his mindset of ignoring his own problems and being ruthless and unforgiving when it comes to other people's wrongs.
      "Will the whole world know your name
      When we dance with destiny?
      I've been there, and I've seen it
      I'm never gonna stop believing
      You'll find your flame
      Oh, we can save the day (the day!)"
    • The chorus represents his goal. At the root of it all, the Flame Knight wanted to be a hero, to be known and respected.
      "It's like I'm seeing double vision
      Take a seat, I'm on a mission
      We got everything to gain (everything to gain, yeah)
      To become everything you're not
      You just give it all you got
      And take the pleasure with the pain"
    • The opening to the second verse kinda shows how the sword is distorting his way of thinking. "Seeing Double Vision" inspired the concept of Flint almost seeing things in two separate ways: his point of view and the way the sword wanted him to see it.
    • "We got everything to gain" along with "We" implying the perspective of two creatures as one (a parasite and its host), shows the sense of drive the Sword instilled in Flint to accomplish his goals no matter what.
    • "You just give it all you got and take the pleasure with the pain." It's self-explanatory, but once again, it is the sword reiterating that the ends justify the means and that he'll be a hero if he just does what he has to.
      "You know I'll turn you into rust, grind you right down to dust
      You thought you were the best, well, you haven't met us"
    • Finally, These lyrics represent how Flint view the party themselves. The party are what Flint aspired to be and he hates that. He wanted to be a hero more than anything, but for some reason you all are able to in a way he couldn't. It became a competition to him, and began to think that you all thought incredibly highly of yourselves simply because of how much he wanted to be like you. "You haven't met us" refers once again to the idea that they are two beings acting in tandem, in what Flint believes is a mutualistic relationship, but is very clearly a parasitic one.
  • "With Me" by Crush 40 serves as Geralt's theme. I actually found this theme after planning out his character, but it happened to line up incredibly well with him and his motivations. But since you all don't know too much about him, I figured instead of explaining the lyrics, I'd just highlight the ones I thought fit very well.
    • "I am (I am)
      Who you don't think I am (think I am)
      All wrapped up
      In my evil plan"
    • "You know (you know)
      Every world will have its test (have its test)
      Don't blame (don't blame)
      For what I have become"
    • "You know (you know)
      Every world will come to end (come to end)
      And I'll (and I'll)
      I'll create your final rest"
    • "I can taste the day (taste the day)
      Savor night (savor night)
      Scream your dreams as you dare to fight
      Dare to fight!"
  • "Rivers in the Desert" from Persona 5 was used as theme when you traversed through your individual nightmares, because the theme of the song is doing the impossible. Just as it is impossible to create a River in a Desert, it can feel impossible to overcome your past.

Thematic Relevance
...

I've used a lot of songs in the campaign that don't have lyrics as well. Many of these were picked because of the original intent of the song and how that applies to the situation I used it in.

  • A big thing you'll notice with me is my love of leitmotif. Essentially the reuse of a theme in different ways to evoke a character, idea or situation.
    • You'll notice for example when you encounter Shank and Dagger that they have an encounter theme and when you fight them a more intense song with the same melody plays.
    • Similarly when you fought Flint for the first time a song played that gave off an anxious, weak vibe. When he picked up the Sword of Darkness and when he revealed himself to be the Flame Knight, the song that played was menacing and evil sounding but had the same melody.
    • While I haven't used this a ton in the campaign thus far I can promise more and more examples of it will begin to pop-up as we go on.
  • When you fought the nightmare in Mufa's dream that manifested itself into the shape of you're nightmares the song that played was "I'll Face Myself-Battle" from Persona 4. The theme of the bosses in Persona 4 is facing the parts of yourself that you are afraid or ashamed of. While different I believe the song's atmosphere equally fit the idea of facing your past.
